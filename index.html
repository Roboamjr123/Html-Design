Islam

Iglesia ni Cristo (Church of Christ)

Iglesia ni Cristo (Church of Christ) was officially founded on July 27, 1914, in the Philippines by Felix Y. Manalo. Felix Manalo is regarded as the church's first Executive Minister. The Iglesia ni Cristo is a Christian denomination that emerged in the early 20th century and has since grown to become one of the largest religious organizations in the Philippines.

Aglipayan Church (Iglesia Filipina Independiente)

The Iglesia Filipina Independiente (Aglipayan Church) was officially established on August 3, 1902, in the Philippines. It was founded as a result of the Philippine struggle for independence from Spanish colonial rule and the desire for a national church independent of foreign control.

Roman Catholicism

The Philippines is known for having a strong Catholic influence, with a significant majority of the population being Roman Catholic. The Spanish colonization in the 16th century played a crucial role in the introduction and spread of Catholicism in the country.

Protestantism

Various Protestant denominations have also gained a following in the Philippines. This includes groups such as Evangelicals, Baptists, Methodists, and others. The influence of Protestantism has grown over the years, and there are many Protestant churches and communities throughout the country.

Islam

Islam is the second-largest religion in the Philippines, and it is particularly prevalent in the southern regions of Mindanao, Sulu Archipelago, and Palawan. The majority of Filipino Muslims belong to the Sunni branch of Islam.

Iglesia ni Cristo (Church of Christ)

Iglesia ni Cristo is a Christian denomination that originated in the Philippines in the early 20th century. It has a significant following in the country and has expanded internationally. The Iglesia ni Cristo has a unique doctrinal perspective and organizational structure.

Aglipayan Church (Iglesia Filipina Independiente)

The Aglipayan Church is a Christian denomination in the Philippines that separated from the Roman Catholic Church in the early 20th century. It is sometimes referred to as the Philippine Independent Church and is known for its distinct nationalistic and independent stance.
